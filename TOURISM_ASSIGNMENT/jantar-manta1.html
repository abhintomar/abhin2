About mussoori

Ah, Mussoorie! Nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas, it's often referred to as the "Queen of the Hills." This hill station in Uttarakhand, India, is known for its lush landscapes, pleasant weather, and colonial charm. Whether you're looking to take a leisurely stroll along the Mall Road, visit the stunning Kempty Falls, or simply enjoy the breathtaking views from Lal Tibba, Mussoorie has something for everyone.

History

Mussoorie has a rich history that dates back to the early 19th century.
Gorkha Rule: In 1803, the Gorkhas under Umer Singh Thapa conquered the Garhwal region, including Mussoorie. British Era: The British established Mussoorie as a hill station in 1825. Captain Frederick Young, a British military officer, played a significant role in its development2
. Great Trigonometric Survey: In 1832, Mussoorie was the intended terminus of the Great Trigonometric Survey of India.
British Retreat: The British used Mussoorie as a summer retreat to escape the heat of the plains. Many colonial-era buildings and institutions still stand today1.
